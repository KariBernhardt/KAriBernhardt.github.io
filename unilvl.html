Growth at the University Level

Influential Benedictine Values

  • Community
    “Striving together for the common good and growing in relationship with God, one another, and self.”
    • The Benedictine Value of Community resonates deeply with me for several reasons. Firstly, I view the entire healthcare system as a vast, interconnected community working together to provide exceptional patient care. It has been a privilege to be a part of this community for several years now, as it has allowed me to discover my passion for helping others in ways that I would not have thought possible. Additionally, I am proud to have been a member of the community of student scholars at the University of Mary, where I learned the value of collaborative work towards academic and patient care goals. Being a part of a community has also taught me the importance of supporting each other through difficult times and celebrating each other's successes.
  • Respect for Persons
    “Recognizing the image of God in each person and honoring each one in their giftedness and limitations”

    • During my time at the University of Mary, I have found the Benedictine Value of Respect for Persons to be highly influential. This value acknowledges the inherent worth and individuality of each person. As a student in the healthcare field, I have witnessed the positive impact of inclusive and collaborative work environments that prioritize empathy, understanding, and compassion towards both colleagues and patients. Furthermore, I have seen noticeable improvement in communication, teamwork, and productivity. I have personally experienced the benefits of appreciating this Benedictine value, which has led me to become more receptive to diverse perspectives and fostered my ability to innovate when it comes to problem-solving and decision-making.
  • Service
    “Meeting the needs of other in the example of Jesus the servant leader”

    • The Benedictine Value of Service has been a driving force in my personal and professional development, particularly in my healthcare career. My clinical experiences have taught me how to provide care for individuals from diverse backgrounds, regardless of their socioeconomic status or cultural beliefs. Through these experiences, I have come to understand the significance of providing high-quality care with kindness and compassion, as it has a significant impact on a patient's overall experience. I believe that as healthcare providers, we should practice servant leadership, which involves placing the needs of our patients above our own and utilizing our gifts and talents to serve others. Additionally, the value of time cannot be underestimated in healthcare.Investing time in developing strong relationships with our patients is critical in providing them with personalized care that is tailored to their specific needs. The Benedictine Value of Service has played a significant role in shaping my perspective as a healthcare provider and has inspired me to approach my work with compassion, kindness, and a deep commitment to serving others.

    University of Mary Graduate Competencies

  • Communication
    • “Graduates demonstrate excellence in communication. Students are engaging and effective communicators demonstrated by employing collaborative dialogue and adapting language to resonate with multiple audiences and/or cultures. Their communication reflects a strong command of their fields(s) due to the accurate application of disciplinary standards and infusion of disciplinary frameworks and evidence into oral and written works.”

    My graduate education has placed great emphasis on the development of effective communication skills, which I have acquired through various classroom exercises and lectures. Through clinical rotations, I was able to apply and enhance my communication skills further. As an employee and leader, my competency in communication will be a valuable asset, allowing me to communicate effectively with patients, families, colleagues, and other healthcare providers. Moreover, my adeptness in communication will contribute to better patient outcomes, heightened patient satisfaction, and improved overall quality of care within any organization where I work.
  • Moral Courage

    • “Graduates clarify, support, or defend what is right and true, whether reached by faith or reason, when confronted by opposing actions or viewpoints in order to serve an understanding of the truth and to promote a culture of human dignity, justice, and virtue. Students, motivated by serving truth, evaluate and respond to actions or viewpoints opposed to what is right or true. Based on faith or reason, they clarify, support, or defend truth and a culture of human dignity, justice, and virtue.”

    The nurse practitioner program employs case studies, ethical scenarios, and group discussions to underscore the significance of ethical decision-making, critical thinking, and advocacy for marginalized and vulnerable patients. As a student, I was encouraged to cultivate my moral courage by reflecting on my practice and analyzing it critically. By having moral courage, I will be able to stand up for my patients, protect their rights, and challenge any unethical practices in the workplace. Moreover, it will enable me to act in the best interests of my patients and the community, even if it entails discomfort or unpopularity.
  • Professional Distinction

    • “Graduates are servant leaders committed to excellence in their professions and communities. Students integrate knowledge and skills in the applicable discipline for professional service and/or contributions to society. They integrate applicable ethical principles and synthesize supporting reasons/evidence for decision-making. Students pursue the common good by prioritizing the needs of, and cultivating the growth of, others above their own circumstances and aspirations.”

    The curriculum included courses on leadership, healthcare policy, and healthcare delivery systems. I was encouraged to pursue professional development opportunities, such as attending conferences and joining professional organizations. Maintaining professional distinction is crucial for me as an employee and leader in contributing to healthcare. As a leader, I can model this behavior for my team, creating a culture of professionalism and continuous learning that ultimately leads to improved patient outcomes and better quality of care.
  • Scholarship

    • “Graduates conduct research or evidence-based practice to develop degree-appropriate knowledge of scholarship. Students display the skills and dispositions essential to effectively conduct evidence-based practice or research and produce scholarship. They develop a professional philosophy grounded in scholarship.”

    As part of developing the competency of scholarship, I completed courses on research methods, statistics, and evidence-based practice. I also completed secondary research and presented my findings at the University’s colloquium. Utilizing research to inform clinical decision-making allows me to promote the use of evidence-based practice within the workplace. By continuously enhancing my knowledge and skills in this way, I can keep up with emerging trends and practices in healthcare. This, in turn, enables me to provide the best possible care to my patients and contribute to the overall advancement of healthcare.

    Demonstrating the Graduate Competencies

  • Opinion Editorial: Communication Competency

    • To effectively communicate my thoughts and ideas on the behavioral health crisis in America, I was tasked with writing a persuasive and compelling editorial. This required me to conduct research, analyze various perspectives, and express my own viewpoint in a clear and concise manner. Through this process, I was able to not only enhance my writing skills but also improve my critical thinking and argumentation abilities, which are crucial aspects of effective communication.

  • Food Insecurity Photo Essay: Moral Courage Competency
    • Creating a photo essay on food insecurity required me to confront a sensitive issue and demonstrate moral courage by challenging existing norms and beliefs. Through this impactful medium, I raised awareness and inspired action on this pervasive and systemic problem affecting millions worldwide. This assignment also showcased my commitment to ethical and responsible healthcare practices.

  • Labor and Delivery Policy Review: Professional Distinction Competency
    • This assignment involved assessing and improving a policy to enhance care quality, demonstrating my professionalism, ethical leadership, critical thinking, research, and communication skills. Successfully evaluating policies and proposing evidence-based solutions require a thorough grasp of policy objectives and outcomes, and the ability to identify gaps.

  • Acetaminophen Overdose: Scholarship Competency
    • This presentation required me to conduct in-depth research on medication overdose, gathering and analyzing relevant data and information. Through this assignment, I demonstrate my ability to think critically and to analyze complex information while identifying patterns, draw conclusions, and communicate my findings in a clear and concise manner. This showcases my ability to engage in scholarly inquiry and my commitment to the pursuit of knowledge.
